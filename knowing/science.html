Science & Chemistry

Air

The invisible gas all around us that we breathe

Oxygen

The gas in air that humans and animals need to live

Helium

A light gas that makes balloons float in the air

Steam

Hot water vapor that rises when water boils

Smoke

Gray or black gas that comes from fire

Water

A clear liquid we drink and need to live

Oil

A thick liquid used for cooking and fuel

Juice

Liquid squeezed from fruits like oranges and apples

Milk

A white liquid from cows that makes bones strong

Honey

A sweet, thick liquid made by bees

Ice

Frozen water that is hard and cold

Rock

A hard, solid piece of the earth

Wood

The hard material that comes from trees

Metal

Hard, shiny materials like iron, steel, and aluminum

Glass

A hard, clear material you can see through

Gold

A precious yellow metal used for jewelry and coins

Silver

A shiny white metal used in jewelry and mirrors

Iron

A strong, dark metal used for building and tools

Copper

A reddish metal used in wires and pennies

Aluminum

A lightweight, silvery metal used for cans and foil

Diamond

The hardest natural crystal, very precious and sparkly

Quartz

A clear, shiny crystal found in rocks

Salt

White crystals used to flavor food

Sugar

Sweet white crystals used to make food taste sweet

Ice Crystal

Beautiful patterns made by frozen water
